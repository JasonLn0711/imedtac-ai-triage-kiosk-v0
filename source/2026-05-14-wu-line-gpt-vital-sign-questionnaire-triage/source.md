## Context Summary

The GPT answer recommends a `家醫科 / 一般內科` or primary-care-style
pre-visit triage product rather than starting with kidney, urology, cardiology,
or pulmonology as the first commercial specialty. The rationale is that the
available measurements are broad vital signs rather than specialty-specific
tests such as urine testing, ECG interpretation, creatinine/eGFR, pulmonary
function testing, or imaging.

The answer frames the product as a `Smart Primary Care Triage Station` /
`AI 家醫分流站` / `智慧門診前評估站` that combines:

- measured height, weight, BMI, heart rate, respiratory rate, SpO2,
  temperature, and blood pressure,
- ASR-supported symptom questioning,
- LLM symptom structuring and summary generation,
- rule-engine red-flag and routing logic,
- user-facing triage support output,
- clinician-facing SBAR-style summary.

## Product Signals Worth Preserving

- The proposed commercial starting point is an entrance triage workflow for
  primary care / general internal medicine, not a single-organ diagnostic
  system.
- The proposed MVP emphasizes acute symptom triage, respiratory/fever triage,
  chronic-risk entry, and specialty referral routing.
- The suggested architecture keeps the LLM in communication, extraction,
  follow-up, and summary roles while reserving red-flag routing for a rule
  engine.
- The answer proposes a 10-question condensed intake flow and a four-level
  routing output: emergency red flag, same-day care, general outpatient care,
  and health tracking.
- The answer includes draft adult numeric thresholds for SpO2, respiratory
  rate, heart rate, blood pressure, temperature, and BMI.

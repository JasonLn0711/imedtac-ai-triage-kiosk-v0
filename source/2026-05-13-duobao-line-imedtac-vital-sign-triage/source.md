# 2026-05-13 Duobao LINE - imedtac Vital-Sign Triage Clinical Calibration

after sharing the previous day's AI-Triage meeting record and Johnny Fang's

## Clinical Takeaways

- 多寶 is interested in this project and had previously wanted to try building
  an emergency-triage system.
- If vital signs are available, the first strong use case is emergency triage;
  urology is less directly driven by vital signs.
- Emergency triage uses history taking plus vital signs. Vital signs can be more
  important than the questionnaire in some cases.
- Unstable vital signs directly raise urgency; examples mentioned were low
  blood pressure and very fast heart rate.
- For a kiosk user who can stand and measure vital signs, signal use depends on
  specialty and workflow rather than implying immediate danger.
- Different specialties care about different signals; internal medicine usually
  cares strongly about vital signs.
- Core vital-sign set mentioned: heart rate, respiration, blood pressure, and
  body temperature.

## Urology Notes

- Many urology diseases have limited vital-sign impact.
- BPH / prostate enlargement is common among older men.
- Blood pressure can matter because some urology medications can lower blood
  pressure.
- Stones are common; pain can raise heart rate, but stone status usually needs
  ultrasound or CT.
- Some chronic-disease / vital-sign concerns may be handled by internal
  medicine rather than urology.

## Execution Implication

- Friday discussion should not claim one universal vital-sign logic across all
  specialties.
- The strongest near-term product story is emergency / internal-medicine-style
  triage support, with urology kept as a structured-intake reference rather than
  the main vital-sign-driven use case.
- Narrow one category / flow before expanding to all-specialty coverage.
- Do not set thresholds, red-flag wording, or final urgency levels without
  clinical review.
